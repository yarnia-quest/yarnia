How a bedtime goes

Tonight

Open the app, and the screen goes dark

No glowing iPad to pry away at lights-out. You open Yarnia, the screen fades to black, and a warm voice takes over the room. You get to just sit with your kid in the dark.

In the story

Your kid becomes the hero of the story

Not a stock tale with their name dropped in, but a story built around this kid: the characters they love, the world they want to be in. The dragon they adore is in it. The dark they fear is not.

Tomorrow night

The story remembers them

Yarnia carries what matters from one night to the next: the favorite characters, the fears to steer around, and what helped them drift off last night. It knows your kid the way a grandparent who knows them would.

Most personalized story apps keep your kid looking at a screen

We built Yarnia to do the opposite. We made it for one thing: this kid, at 8pm, with the screen off. The story is theirs and grows with them, and it remembers your actual kid, not just a recurring cast of characters. No catalog to scroll, no illustrations to lean into, no new app to add to the pile. Just a warm voice in the dark, made for that hard hour at the end of the day.

And when tonight's story lands just right, you will be able to send it to grandma or a co-parent who is not in the room, so the people who love your kid can hear it too.

Every story runs through age and safety checks before your child hears it, and is shaped to steer around the things that scare them, so you do not have to preview each one yourself.
