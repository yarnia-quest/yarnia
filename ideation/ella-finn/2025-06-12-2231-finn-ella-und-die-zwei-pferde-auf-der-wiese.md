## Story Summary

On a warm summer day, Finn and Ella walk to the big meadow beyond the creek. They cross the small stream, climb the hill, and find two horses grazing.

One horse is white with a long mane. The other is brown with light patches on its forehead. Their names are Luma and Rocco.

Finn has never seen such large animals so close. Ella politely asks whether they may watch them.

The horses explain that they come to the meadow in summer because the grass is good. Finn tries a bite of grass and decides moss is more his thing.

Luma and Rocco tell old horse stories about night rides under stars, wind in manes, and rain washing paths clean. Finn and Ella listen under an oak tree and grow sleepy.

They return slowly to the forest house while the horses continue grazing.
