## Slide 1 — Problem + customer
- **Headline:** It's 8pm. The kid won't settle. The parent reaches for the iPad they'll regret.
- Who: parents of ~3–7 year olds. A moment that happens every single night.
- Today they either hand over a screen (guilt) or fight bedtime.

## Slide 2 — Solution + product
- **Headline:** Yarnia: open it, the screen goes dark, a voice tells your kid a story made just for them.
- Light co-creation up front ("an owl or a dragon?"), then a calm narrated story to sleep.
- *(Live demo / QR here. This slide is mostly the demo.)*

## Slide 3 — Why now
- **Headline:** This is only possible in 2026.
- Real-time expressive voice (ElevenLabs) + cheap generation make a conversational, memory-driven bedtime ritual work now.
- Plus the anti-attention moment: parents actively want *less* screen, not more.

## Slide 4 — Market + competition
- **Headline:** Sleep apps play the same recording for every kid. We don't.
- Real incumbents: **Moshi, Yoto, Toniebox, Calm Kids.** (Name them. Never say "no competitors.")
- Why they can't follow: they're pre-recorded catalogs / fixed cards. Yarnia is generative + per-child memory. Different product shape, costly for a catalog/kid-safety business to retrofit.
- Bottom-up: # parents × €8/mo. (Fill the real number.)

## Slide 5 — Business model + evidence
- **Model:** €8/mo subscription (Calm €8, Spotify €10 — a price you say without hesitation).
- **Evidence:** landing-page signups [screenshot the count] + any "I'd pay for this" quote, verbatim.
- Content safety as trust: every story runs through age + safety constraints, and avoids the child's known fears.

## Slide 6 — Go-to-market
- **Headline:** The story sells the next parent.
- Channel 1: the share loop — "send tonight's story to grandma" → grandma hears it → new parent at the top of the funnel.
- Channel 2: parent communities + preschool/daycare partnerships.

## The one demo moment that wins
Center the demo on **memory**: Yarnia opens already knowing the child ("Another dragon story like last time? I'll keep the thunder out."). That is the single thing Moshi/Yoto structurally cannot do. Seed it so it's reliable. Record a 60–90s backup video in case the venue wifi or live voice fails.
