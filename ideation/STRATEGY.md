## Mission & hard constraints

## North star — the judging rubric (this IS the spec)
| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| Problem & customer clarity | 20% |
| Market & business potential | 20% |
| Product execution & demo | 20% |
| AI-native leverage & technical approach | 15% |
| Evidence, insight & founder edge | 15% |
| Pitch clarity | 10% |

**40% is buyer + business; only 20% is the demo.** A rough product with a razor-sharp buyer beats a glossy demo with no buyer logic.

## How to win (the 6 moves)
1. **Name one buyer in one painful moment.** *"We help [specific customer] do [specific painful job] because today they're stuck with [bad workaround]."*
2. **Sell the work, not a copilot.** The product *does the job*.
3. **Narrow wedge, not a platform.** One workflow, end-to-end; show how it grows into a system of record.
4. **Bring one non-copyable evidence point.** A stranger saying "take my money" in writing. Compliments ≠ evidence.
5. **Bottom-up market math:** # customers × revenue/customer. Never "1% of a $50B market."
6. **Name your own weakest point** + the test you'd run next.

## 7-slide deck structure
1. Problem + customer · 2. Solution + product (demo) · 3. Why now · 4. Market + competition (bottom-up; never "no competitors") · 5. Business model + evidence · 6. Go-to-market · 7. Team + contact.
(Yarnia's drafted slides: `DECK.md`.)

## Guardrails — do NOT build these
**Weak-idea patterns:** ChatGPT-for-X wrapper · "AI [job title]" with no named human · feature-not-company · big-number-no-plan · solution-looking-for-a-problem · generic horizontal "for everyone" · second-mover-with-no-edge.
**Crowded categories:** generic chatbots · note-takers · resume builders · AI companions · generic SEO/content · code-gen copilots · generic LLM observability.
